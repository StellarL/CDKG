    Connection Between the Order of Katugampola Fractional Integral and Fractal Dimensions of Weierstrass Function

    ZHANG Xia PENG Wen-liang

    Nanjing University of Science and Technology Army University of Engineering of PLA

    Abstract：

    We investigate fractal dimensions of Katugampola fractional integral of Weierstrass function defined on a closed interval. Fractal dimensions such as Box dimension, K-dimension and Packing dimension are calculated. Furthermore, we get the result that there exists some linear relationship between the order of Katugampola fractional integral and fractal dimensions of Weierstrass function.

    Keyword：

    Katugampola fractional integral; Fractal dimensions; Weierstrass function;

    Received： 2018-05-13

    1 Introduction

    Fractal calculus has been used to explore fractal function as an important tool such as Weyl-Marchaud fractal derivative[1], Riemann-Liouville fractal integral[2]. Most people made research on relationship between fractional integral and fractal dimensions[3].

    The present paper mainly investigates Box dimension of Katugampola fractional integral of Weierstrass function. Firstly, we give Box dimension and Katugampola fractional integral as follows.

    Deinition 1[4] Let F (F≠∅) be a bounded subset of ℝn and let Nδ (F) be the smallest number of sets of diameter at most δ which can cover F. Lower Box dimension and upper Box dimension of F, respectively are defined as follows:

    dim¯B (F) =lim¯δ→0logΝδ (F) -logδ

    and

    dimB¯ (F) =limδ→0¯logΝδ (F) -logδ.

    If the above two are equal, we call the common value as Box dimension of F. That is

    dimB (F) =limδ→0logΝδ (F) -logδ.

    Remark 2[4] For a subset Fof ℝn, we have

    0≤dimΗ (F) ≤dim¯B (F) ≤dimB¯ (F) ≤2.

    Definition 1.3[5] Let f (x) be defined on [0, 1]. Katugampola fractional integral of f (x) is defined as

    0ρΙ+vf (x) = (ρ+1) 1-vΓ (v) ∫0x (xρ+1-tρ+1) v-1tρf (t) dt,

    where v>0 and ρ≠-1 are real number.
